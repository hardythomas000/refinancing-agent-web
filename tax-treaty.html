The Double Tax Agreement (DTA)

How It Works

Australia and Indonesia have a Double Tax Agreement that prevents you from being taxed twice on the same income. Here's how it applies to your situation:

üá¶üá∫ Australian Rental Income

  • Taxed in Australia first
  • Must lodge Australian tax return
  • Can claim all property deductions
  • Pay at resident or non-resident rates

üáÆüá© Indonesian Requirements

  • If Indo tax resident (183+ days)
  • Declare worldwide income
  • Get credit for Australian tax paid
  • 4-year exemption may apply

Foreign Tax Credit

The key mechanism is the foreign tax credit:

  • If you pay tax on rental income in Australia, you get a credit in Indonesia
  • You don't pay tax twice on the same income
  • The country where income is sourced (Australia) has first taxing rights on property income

2026 Tax Residency Rules

The "Ceasing Residency" Test

Under the new rules effective July 2026, to become a non-resident for tax, you must satisfy ALL of these:

‚ùå Employment Contract

No 2+ year contract means you stay a resident. Staying a resident is BETTER for your tax.

‚úÖ Overseas Accommodation

Secured accommodation in Indonesia. Assumed yes.

‚úÖ Family Relocating

Spouse and children moving with you. Yes (wife and baby).

‚úÖ

This Is Actually Good News

Since you don't qualify as a non-resident, you'll remain an Australian tax resident. This means you get the $18,200 tax-free threshold and lower marginal rates on your rental income!

What happens after 2 years?

If you stay in Indonesia for more than 2 years AND secure a long-term job contract, the ATO will likely consider you a non-resident from that point onwards.

  • The Change: You lose the tax-free threshold.
  • The Impact: Your rental income tax increases to a flat 30%.
  • The Strategy: Enjoy the resident rates for the first 2 years. If your stay becomes permanent, we need to adjust your tax planning.

Indonesian Tax Obligations

NPWP Registration

If you become an Indonesian tax resident (183+ days in 12 months), you may need to register for an NPWP (Nomor Pokok Wajib Pajak - Tax Identification Number).

Required Documents

  • Valid passport with visa (KITAS/KITAP preferred)
  • Proof of Indonesian address (rental contract)
  • KITAS or KITAP (Stay Permit)
  • Letter of employment (if working)

4-Year Exemption

Indonesia offers a potential 4-year exemption on foreign-sourced income for new expats:

  • May apply to your Australian rental income
  • Discuss with Indonesian tax advisor upon arrival
  • Conditions and eligibility vary

You Lose Main Residence Exemption When You Rent It Out

From the day you start renting your property, CGT applies when you eventually sell. However, the 6-year absence rule may provide partial protection if you move back in before selling.

Example CGT Calculation

If you sell for $750k after 2 years of renting:

Purchase price (estimated) $600,000
Sale price $750,000
Capital gain $150,000
50% discount (held >12 months) ‚àí$75,000
Taxable capital gain $75,000
Tax at marginal rate (~37%) ~$27,750

Strategies to Minimize CGT

  • Keep property 1-2 years after returning to Australia (if you move back in)
  • Maintain good records of all improvements (add to cost base)
  • Get professional advice before selling
  • Use the 6-year absence rule if you return within 6 years

Non-Resident CGT Withholding

If you're a non-resident when selling:

  • 15% withholding on the sale price (increased from 12.5% in 2025)
  • Sale price $750k = $112,500 withheld at settlement
  • You still lodge tax return to calculate actual CGT
  • May get refund if actual CGT is less than withholding
