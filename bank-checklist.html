Be Honest But Strategic

When talking to lenders, be upfront about your situation. Honesty protects you legally, and rental income can actually boost your serviceability.

Key Talking Points

  • "I'm moving overseas temporarily (1-2 years) and converting my home to a rental property."
  • "I want to refinance to an investment loan and add $15k cash-out for relocation costs."
  • "My current LVR is 42%‚ÄîI have excellent equity in the property."
  • "I have a rental appraisal showing expected weekly rent of $670/week ($2,900/month)."

Why This Helps

  • Rental income counts: Banks typically use 80% of expected rental income for serviceability
  • Your wife not working: Less impactful when rental income is factored in
  • Low LVR: 42% puts you in a strong negotiating position
  • Temporary move: Shows you intend to return (better than permanent emigration)

Refinance while you still have Australian employment. Once overseas with no local income, applications become much harder. Aim to complete refinancing 3 months before departure.

‚ùì Questions To Ask Lenders

  • "What's your investment variable rate for my LVR (42%)?"
    Push for discount‚Äîyour low LVR is strong leverage
  • "Include the $15k relocation cash-out in my application"
    Confirm maximum cash-out available
  • "What fees apply to convert/refinance?"
    Application fee, valuation, settlement, discharge
  • "Will my overseas move affect loan approval?"
    Some lenders more expat-friendly than others
  • "Does the investment loan include an offset account?"
    Rent payments can sit here to reduce interest daily
  • "What's the typical approval timeline?"
    Plan for 2-4 weeks; some are faster
  • "Are there any cashback offers currently available?"
    Some lenders offer up to $5k for refinancing
